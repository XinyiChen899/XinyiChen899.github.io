📝 Selected Projects & Research
JudgmentLens: Interactive Visualization for Legal Judgment Comprehension
Xinyi Chen, Ruijie Li
Target: CHI 2027 · In Progress
We present JudgmentLens, a multi-modal visualization system addressing cognitive barriers in legal judgment reading. Through formative study (questionnaire N=34, interview N=6), we identified three barrier types: evidence recognition, statutory citation, and judicial reasoning. The system provides timeline view, evidence contrast diagram, logic reasoning tree, layered reading (plain-language toggle), party-switching perspective, and traceable AI explanations.
📊 Comprehension: +42% ⏱ Task time: −35% 🎯 SUS: 82.4 📐 Cohen's d: ≥ 1.5 👥 N=16 user study

From Immigrant to Native: Workflow Patterns of AI-Generated Narrative Video Creators
Yuelu Li, Xinyi Chen, Chengcheng Zhao
Target: CSCW 2026 · Manuscript in Preparation
We conducted semi-structured interviews (N=22) with AI narrative video creators on Chinese platforms, covering serial-delivery, premium-project, and account-operation creator types. Through inductive thematic analysis of 304-page transcripts, we identified three workflow patterns and propose a theoretical framework distinguishing "AIGC Immigrants" vs. "AIGC Natives"—revealing divergent understandings of creative control, tool collaboration, and quality standards.
👥 N=22 interviews 📄 304 pp transcript 🔍 3 workflow models 💡 New theory: T2/T5 framework

When to Speak Up: User Study on Proactive AI Agent Intervention Timing
Xinyi Chen
Course Research Project · HKUST(GZ) · 2024
Designed and conducted a within-subjects user experiment (N=32) evaluating proactive AI agent intervention timing. Collected SUS, NASA-TLX, and behavioral logs; applied repeated-measures ANOVA and LMM. Found that premature proactive suggestions increased cognitive load by 27%. Recommendations led to algorithm adjustments (confidence threshold, trigger delay), improving task completion rate from 68% to 81%.
👥 N=32 📈 68%→81% completion 🧠 Cognitive load −27% 📐 ANOVA + LMM
